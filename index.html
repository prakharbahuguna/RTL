Latest News

RTL have just shipped another full thermal and corrosion scanner to South Korea. This is the sixth scanner to be installed in this country and the second on this modern 'supercritical powered' station that provides electricity for the capital, Seoul. The scanner comes complete with the latest monitoring software and analysis package which sends on-line thermal and corrosion data to the plant control room.

A thermal only scanner extension has been supplied to Drax power station for their Unit 4 boiler. This greatly increases the capability of the existing thermal scanner so that combustion conditions that result in tube rupture can be successfully monitored and eventually overcome.

RTL have recently completed the design and installation of cathodic protection systems to a church tower in Shropshire and to 18th century muses at Seaton Delaval Hall – a National Trust property. Seaton Delaval Hall contains six muses with wrought iron armatures, all of which have been suffering from ongoing corrosion which had cracked and delaminated the plaster/lime bodies and limbs of the sculptures. This is the first time that RTL have applied cathodic protection to plaster and lime statues.

Rowan Technologies has recently been awarded a patent for the Electrical Resistance Measurement Systems which provide the foundations for both the Thermal and Corrosion Monitoring Scanners.

Industrial Monitoring

We design and supply systems for monitoring both the integrity (corrosion, erosion and cracking) and thermal behaviour of industrial plants. Our scanner technology, designed to monitor and map large areas of plant such as boiler walls, has been used in power generation plant since 1999 - our first full-scale system is now set to have an operational life of more than ten years.

Protection & Conservation

We provide services and systems for corrosion risk assessment, prevention and control, and specialise in the design and supply of cathodic protection systems to inhibit the corrosion of embedded metalwork in masonry and concrete. As advisers to English Heritage, we have extensive experience in the preservation and conservation of historic structures, including some of the UK’s best known buildings.
