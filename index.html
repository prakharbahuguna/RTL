Product Focus and Latest News

New Mini-Scanner Systems:

Mini-scanners complement our existing multi-enclosure scanner technology but are designed for smaller monitoring areas, providing both corrosion and heat flux monitoring and mapping capabilities. All the mini-scanner electronics is housed in a single easy-install enclosure.

New Heat Flux and Corrosion (HFC) Monitors:

These low-cost, stand-alone units are designed for both heat flux and corrosion monitoring at point locations on boiler membrane walls and other surfaces. They use the principle of electrical resistance measurement to accurately determine the real heat flux and any loss-in-thickness due to corrosion or erosion.

Sensors are simply welded to external tube walls as opposed to intrusive heat flux systems that require tube removal and replacement.

Latest News

Industrial Monitoring

Two of RTL's new monitoring systems have now been installed on a sub-critical biofuel boiler at Drax, UK. These particular systems combine the capabilities of the HFC monitors and mini-scanner systems so providing both multi-point heat flux and corrosion monitoring. These systems form part of an upgrade to an existing scanner system that was originally commissioned in 2001. A further HFC monitor has been supplied for use on a supercritical boiler in Texas, USA.

Upgrades to the biofuel boiler at Drax included the installation of five air-cooled VTER probes for monitoring the corrosion rates of the superheater and reheater tubing.

Commissioning of a thermal and corrosion scanner system for an 800 MW supercritical boiler at Yeungheung power plant, South Korea, is soon to get underway.

Industrial Monitoring

We design and supply a range of systems for monitoring both the integrity (corrosion, erosion and cracking) and thermal behaviour of industrial plants. Our scanner technology, designed to monitor and map large areas of plant such as boiler walls, has been used in power generation plant since 1999. New in 2015, 'mini-scanners' now complement these larger systems and are designed for smaller monitoring areas. Also introduced in 2015 are our new heat flux monitors, designed for precision monitoring at point locations.
